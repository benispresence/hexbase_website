Staking

Stake length and average principal are on the rise while a couple of whales are quitting and their freshly exited positions are getting staked by core #HEXICANS

42.5 billion HEX was staked in Q2, which is similar to Q1, from which alone $pHEX accounts for 30 billion. We haven't seen these levels since BigPayDay in Q4 2020.

This is a lot of HEX being staked by a core #HEXICAN group, who staked an average of 6.7 million $eHEX and 2.5 million $pHEX. This is a new record on the quarterly chartâ€” not bad!

These stakes are not just big but also loooong. The average stake length is rising to 1,300 days, or 3.6 years. Especially eHEX seems to be growing in length, now for the 3rd quarter in a row!

Penalties

Huge penalties occurred in Q2, similar to Q1, but this time it happened equally with eHEX and pHEX, each having around 900 million HEX in penalties. A total of 1.8 billion in penalties. These penalties are due to a few whales exiting their stakes. This is evident by looking at the Emergency End Staker to Active Staker (EES/AS) ratio. Only 0.23% of eHEX stakers emergency end staked, and 0.44% of pHEX stakers did. This is the lowest ever recorded. See the chart and other ones in the thread below.

Adoption

This past quarter, we had fewer active users for #HEX (this combines eHEX and pHEX) at 41k active users. This is a similar level to Q2 2021 before the big run-up to $0.55.

Furthermore, not many new #HEX users joined this past quarter. Only 9k new users tried out our favorite DEFI protocol.

Usage

Lastly, I have an $eHEX-only chart for the 300k believers about usage. Staking activity on #Ethereum for HEX rose to 24% from 14% in Q1. That is similar to swapping activity, which is 25%. Most of the usage is dominated by administer activity at 34%. Also, MEV or arbitrage bots are rising in overall usage, now occupying 9% of all activity. Due to thicker liquidity and more volume on #Pulsechain, this situation makes sense. But overall, it is great that staking activity is holding up quite well for eHEX.
